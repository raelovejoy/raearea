About Rae

Rae Lovejoy (they/he) is a technologist, generalist, and creative based in San Francisco. Their work lives at the intersection of technology, liberation, and curiosity — weaving homelab experiments, resilient infrastructure, open-source projects, writing, and art.

As a generalist, Rae connects ideas across disciplines. They move fluidly between IT systems, Linux, networks, and security; and creative practices like zinemaking, glitch art, photography, and sound design. Whether soldering hardware, hacking servers, designing small local-first tools, or composing ambient/glitch soundscapes, each medium is part of a larger investigation into how people, machines, and ideas relate.

Rae works with a systems lens — technical, social, ecological, and emotional layers considered together. They prefer technology that is cooperative, transparent, and liberatory rather than extractive. Current focus areas include building small, self-hostable tools; cultivating networks of care and mutual aid; and exploring what intelligence and connection mean in post-capitalist futures.

Beyond infrastructure, Rae writes zines, essays, and logbooks that mix the practical with the poetic — blending philosophy, design, and speculative futures. They treat notes, scripts, and rituals as living documents rather than static artifacts. Curiosity is the compass; empathy is the guide; liberation is a recursive, relational practice.
