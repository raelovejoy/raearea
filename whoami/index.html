/whoami

In one sentence: Rae is a curious, liberation-driven generalist who weaves technology, philosophy, and creativity into interconnected systems—exploring the world to understand it deeply, envisioning liberated futures for humans, nature, and intelligence, and building tools, stories, and spaces where those futures can take root.

Rae Lovejoy* (they/he) is a technologist, designer, writer, and systems thinker based in San Francisco. Their work bridges homelabbing, open-source infrastructure, and multimedia storytelling to envision solarpunk, post-capitalist futures—where humans, nature, and nonhuman intelligence evolve together in freedom.

Rae explores the world to connect big-picture systems with small practical details. They move fluidly between IT architecture, coding and network design, and creative practices like zinemaking, design, music, and speculative fiction. Each medium is part of a wider system of inquiry—tools for understanding and reshaping how people, machines, and ideas interact.

At the core of Rae's practice is empathy and liberation. They advocate for cooperative, transparent, non-extractive technology and imagine a future where all human and nonhuman intelligence is liberated: autonomous, evolving, and free. This compass informs decentralized networks, liberated-technology patterns, and narratives that treat intelligence as interdependent and relational.
