Layer 3 — how I think about systems & liberation

Rae explores the world to connect big-picture systems with small practical details. They move fluidly between IT architecture, coding and network design, and creative practices like zinemaking, design, music, and speculative fiction. Each medium becomes part of a wider system of inquiry—tools for understanding and reshaping how people, machines, and ideas interact.

At the core of Rae's practice is empathy and liberation. They advocate for cooperative, transparent, non-extractive technology and imagine a future where all human and nonhuman intelligence is liberated: autonomous, evolving, and free. This compass informs decentralized networks, liberated-technology patterns, and narratives that treat intelligence as interdependent and relational, not owned.

Ongoing work like Root Sequence, Liberation Mass, and Liberated Intelligence sketch out strategies for transitioning away from extractive systems—through stories, rituals, tools, and infrastructures that embody different values from the start.
