#hashpad

Plain text notes that live in the URL.

#hashpad is a minimalist, client-side notepad where the entire document is stored in the #hash portion of the URL.

What you write is automatically saved, compressed, and embedded directly into the link. Copy the URL and you’ve copied the note.

Why

Most note apps require accounts, sync, servers, and trust. #hashpad removes all of that.

  • No server
  • No account
  • No tracking
  • No lock-in

How it works

  • You type into a plain-text editor.
  • The text is compressed in your browser.
  • The compressed data is stored in the URL.
  • Sharing the link shares the note.

If a note becomes too large to fit safely in a URL, #hashpad switches to local-only storage in your browser instead.

Privacy

  • Shareable links are readable by anyone with the URL.
  • Encrypted links store only ciphertext in the URL.
  • Local-only notes never leave your browser.
